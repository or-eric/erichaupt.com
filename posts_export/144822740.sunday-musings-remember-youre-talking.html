One Quote I’m Musing

“Remember, you’re not talking to a bad man.”

-Cleanthes

This is a very small passage in Diogenes Laertius' “Lives of the Eminent Philosophers. Cleanthes was walking down a street and noticed a man talking negatively to himself he stopped the man and told him “Remember, you’re not talking to a bad man”.

To improve, we have to hold a mirror of truth up and examine ourselves, our flaws, our shortcomings, in addition to our strengths. That’s a part of self-discipline.

But you cannot turn critical evaluation so far as to become self-flagellation, driving down your own self-esteem. It’s not self-punishment, it’s self-improvement.

Be your own friend, when you’ve done that; you will be a better friend to others by nature.

-e

Two Ideas From Me

  1. No one who’s done anything new or unconventional did so without criticisms and haters. The alternative is to just do nothing, and that’s no alternative at all.

  2. Chasing external validation undermines self-approval. By ignoring the dichotomy of control, we make our self-worth fragile and dependent on others instead of rooted in our own values.
